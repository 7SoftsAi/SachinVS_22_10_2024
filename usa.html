    BECOMING A NURSE IN AMERICA

    1. Hold a Diploma or Bachelor's Degree in Nursing
    Nursing diploma should be equivalent to an Associate's Degree in Nursing in the U.S.
    2. Hold Nursing License in Country of Practice
    Nursing license should be a full and unrestricted license to practice nursing
    3. Pass IELTS Exam
    TOEFL iBT, PTE and OET are also accepted for US immigration
    4. Pass NCLEX Exam
    NCLEX is the nationwide exam for the licensing of nurses in the U.S.
    5. Obtain VisaScreen Certificate
    A VisaScreen verifies the nurse’s education and English language skills have been evaluated and found acceptable for US nursing practice
    6. Secure Job Offer from U.S. Healthcare Facility
    Our hospitals, post-acute care facilities, nursing homes, and dialysis centers are currently hiring international nurses
    7. Secure Sponsorship for EB3 Immigrant Visa
    An employment-based immigrant visa also known as a green card allows you to live and work in the US permanently
    8. Attend Immigrant Visa Interview
    Spouse and children will be interviewed along with the RN
    9. Arrive in USA
    We’ll arrange your flight, airport pickup, and accommodation
    10. Work as a USRN
    We’ll help you secure your social security number and nursing license
